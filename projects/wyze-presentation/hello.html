Project

The existing system:

  • Provides health information to help residents manage chronic conditions

  • Information is presented on a printed flyer

Our job:

  • Make the information more accessible by converting it into an easy to use interactive web site

  • Allow users to record and track symptoms over time to help when communicating with their doctors

Example

  • Name: Dale

  • Age: 72 years old

  • Location: Lives alone in Seattle, WA

  • Occupation: retired

  • Chronic conditions: diabetes, partial blindness

Dale has been feeling more tired than usual, and is worried about his health. He has the flyer about his condition that he picked up the last time he visited the Aging and Disability Services offices in Seattle. He is confused by the information on the flyer. He has symptoms that are listed in more than one section on the flyer and after reading it, is still not sure if he should make an appointment with a doctor or if he should call 911.

Our approach

Problem:

  • Users are not able to confidently select the optimal course of action based on the information they are presented.

Solution:

  • Allow users to indicate the symptoms they are currently experiencing, and then present them with clear next steps to follow through an intuitive and accessible web application.

  • What we learned from users

  • What we changed after getting feedback

Key improvements:

  • Website is available online

  • No guesswork. After entering symptoms, user is presented with clear next steps to follow.

  • Track symptoms over time

Conclusion

Dale opens the web site and selects the page for his chronic condition - Diabetes. He selects the symptoms he is currently experiencing and clicks Submit. The symptoms are analyzed and Dale is given the recommendation to make an appointment with his doctor for further analysis.


Since he has been tracking his symptoms using our service, he is able to provide his doctor with a history of key medical metrics over time.
